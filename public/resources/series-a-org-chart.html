01 · Context

Why most Series A marketing teams are built wrong

After working with 20+ Fintech and B2B SaaS companies across Africa and the UK, one pattern keeps repeating: founders hire for execution before strategy, and content before demand. The result is a marketing team that produces a lot but grows little. This template gives you the architecture to avoid that mistake.

01

Strategy First, Execution Second

Your first marketing hire should own positioning and narrative — not just content production. Get the story right before you pour spend into it.

02

One Primary Channel at a Time

Series A is too early to run six channels. Pick one, nail it to unit-economic proof, then expand. This dictates who you hire first.

03

Hire Generalists, Layer Specialists

Months 0–6: hire people who can do multiple things. Months 6–18: layer in specialists once you know which disciplines matter most for your motion.

03 · Roles

Role definitions & success metrics

Each role below is defined by outcomes, not activities. Use these definitions in JDs, performance reviews, and interviews.

Head of Marketing / VP Marketing

Strategic · Hire First
  • Own the brand narrative and positioning
  • Set quarterly OKRs and marketing budget
  • Present pipeline contribution to board
  • Align marketing motion with sales and product
  • Build and manage the marketing team
Key Metric: Pipeline Contribution % & Brand Share of Voice

Growth & Demand Generation Lead

Execution · Hire Second
  • Own paid acquisition across Meta, Google, LinkedIn
  • Drive SEO strategy and topical authority
  • Run conversion rate optimisation on key pages
  • Manage lead nurture and MQL pipeline
  • Report weekly on CAC by channel
Key Metric: Blended CAC, MQL Volume, Pipeline Generated

Content & Brand Lead

Execution · Hire Third
  • Produce thought leadership (articles, reports, POVs)
  • Manage social media presence and community
  • Brief and manage design for brand assets
  • Own PR and media relationships
  • Build the content calendar and editorial guidelines
Key Metric: Organic Traffic, Share of Voice, Media Mentions

Marketing Operations Manager

Systems · Hire Fourth
  • Own HubSpot / CRM setup and hygiene
  • Build and maintain attribution model
  • Create marketing dashboards for CEO and investors
  • Manage marketing tech stack and integrations
  • Run lead scoring and lifecycle workflows
Key Metric: Data Accuracy %, Attribution Coverage, Reporting Velocity
04 · Sequence

18-month hiring roadmap

The sequence below is optimised for B2B Fintech and SaaS companies running a product-led or sales-assisted motion. Adjust based on your primary channel.

Month 0–3
Post-raise, pre-pipeline

Establish the Strategy Layer

Bring on your Head of Marketing or a fractional VP. Their first 90 days: audit the brand, define the ICP, set the narrative, and build the marketing plan.

Head of MarketingFractional CMO (if budget-constrained)
Month 3–6
Pipeline engine on

Activate Demand Generation

Once positioning is set, hire the person who will own demand — whether paid, SEO, or partnerships. Don't hire before the narrative is locked.

Growth LeadDemand Gen Specialist
Month 6–9
Brand compound starts

Build the Content & Brand Function

Now that you have pipeline activity, add the brand layer. A content lead who can write and brief design will compound your demand efforts over time.

Content LeadPart-time Designer
Month 9–18
Pre-Series B clarity

Layer in Operations & Specialists

As volume grows, attribution breaks down. Hire Marketing Ops to build the reporting infrastructure investors will scrutinise at Series B diligence.

Marketing OperationsPR / Comms (optional)Performance Specialist

06 · Signals

Signals it's time to hire

Don't hire on a calendar. Hire when these thresholds are triggered.

→

Head of Marketing

Founder is spending 30%+ of time on marketing decisions. Pipeline is stalling because there's no marketing strategy. You've raised Series A.

→

Growth Lead

You have product-market fit signals, a clear ICP, and a positioning document. Now you need someone to build and run the acquisition engine.

→

Marketing Ops

You're generating 100+ MQLs/month, attribution is murky, and your CRM is a mess. Investors are asking about marketing ROI and you don't have a clean answer.
