How to Use This

Score yourself before you launch

Work through each section with your marketing and product leads. Check items you've completed. Items marked CRITICAL are non-negotiable — missing them post-launch is expensive to fix. Aim for 40+ before any paid launch.

0–25
Not Ready
Significant gaps. Launching now means wasting budget. Fix foundations first.
26–40
Launch with Caution
Soft launch only. Run discovery before paid spend. Address red items in 60 days.
41–50
Launch Ready
Full launch approved. Scale paid with confidence. Review quarterly.
The Checklist

50 Points across 8 categories

1
Market & ICP Definition
7 items
01
Primary ICP defined with at least 3 firmographic attributes (industry, revenue, headcount)
Document your ideal customer profile. Vague ICPs produce vague pipeline.
Critical
02
ICP validated with a minimum of 10 customer discovery interviews
Assumptions are not validation. Talk to real customers.
Critical
03
Buying committee mapped: Champion, Economic Buyer, Technical Buyer, and Blocker identified
B2B decisions are rarely made by one person. Know your full cast.
High
04
Total Addressable Market (TAM) quantified with a credible, cited source
High
05
Top 3 competitor narratives analysed and your differentiation gap documented
High
06
Customer jobs-to-be-done (JTBD) mapped for primary ICP
Understand what customers are hiring your product to do.
Medium
07
Negative ICP profile created — documented reasons why certain customers are wrong for you
Medium
2
Product Readiness
6 items
08
Product-market fit score of 40%+ (Sean Ellis "very disappointed" test or equivalent)
Below 40% = pre-PMF. Marketing spend before PMF is burning cash.
Critical
09
Core use case articulated in one sentence without jargon
If your team can't say it in one sentence, prospects won't understand it either.
Critical
10
Onboarding flow optimised to deliver measurable value within first session or use
High
11
Pricing page live with at least 2 tiers and clear feature differentiation
High
12
Trial, freemium, or demo mechanic defined with conversion metrics tracked
High
13
Top 5 integrations documented and promoted on website
Medium
3
Messaging & Positioning
8 items
14
Category stance defined: are you creating a new category or entering an existing one?
The answer dictates your entire messaging approach and budget allocation.
Critical
15
Hero message (tagline / homepage H1) tested with real prospects and refined
Critical
16
Unique POV on the industry written, reviewed, and documented in a positioning doc
High
17
Positioning document signed off by CEO, Head of Sales, and Head of Product
High
18
"Why now, why us, why different" clearly answered in writing and accessible to all team
High
19
Objection response library built for top 5 sales objections
High
20
Messaging hierarchy established: Category → Company → Product → Feature
Medium
21
Tone of voice guidelines documented and distributed to all content creators
Medium
4
Content & Assets
7 items
22
Homepage conversion-optimised: clear H1, sub, social proof, and primary CTA above the fold
Critical
23
Minimum 2 published case studies — at least 1 per primary ICP vertical
One case study = table stakes. Two = credibility. Three = category presence.
Critical
24
ROI calculator or value quantification tool live on the website
High
25
Demo or product walkthrough video (2–4 minutes) published on website and YouTube
High
26
Sales deck reviewed and iterated with input from your top 3 reps
High
27
Email nurture sequence (5+ emails) live in HubSpot or equivalent CRM
Medium
28
Help centre or knowledge base seeded with 15+ articles covering core use cases
Medium
5
Channel Strategy
6 items
29
1–2 primary acquisition channels identified through experimentation, not assumption
Spreading budget across 5 channels at Series A guarantees mediocrity on all five.
Critical
30
SEO foundation: 100+ pages indexed, Core Web Vitals passing, keyword strategy documented
High
31
LinkedIn Company Page optimised: banner, description, 500+ followers, weekly posting cadence
High
32
Paid playbook defined: budget allocation, creative rotation schedule, and attribution model
High
33
Community or partner channel identified, with an activation plan and owner assigned
Medium
34
Event and conference calendar for the next 6 months planned with sponsor/speaker targets
Medium
6
Sales Alignment
5 items
35
Lead scoring model agreed and documented between Marketing and Sales leadership
Critical
36
MQL definition written, shared, and enforced in CRM — no informal definitions
Critical
37
Marketing-to-Sales SLA signed: response time, lead volume targets, feedback loop defined
High
38
Joint ICP review scheduled monthly with Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success
High
39
Sales enablement library built: battlecards, case studies, objection responses, email templates
High
7
Analytics & Attribution
6 items
40
UTM tagging convention documented, implemented, and enforced across all campaigns
Bad UTMs = broken attribution = budget decisions made blind.
Critical
41
Full funnel reporting live: Traffic → MQL → SQL → Closed Won, with conversion rates at each stage
Critical
42
CAC tracked by channel, not blended only — organic, paid, referral, and partner separated
High
43
LTV:CAC ratio calculated per segment and reviewed monthly by leadership
High
44
Attribution model chosen and documented — first-touch, last-touch, or linear with rationale
High
45
Live dashboard accessible to CEO, VP Sales, and lead investors — updated automatically
Medium
8
Launch Execution
5 items
46
Launch timeline with T-minus milestones built and shared with all stakeholders (including product and engineering)
Critical
47
Press and media outreach list of 15+ journalists, newsletters, and podcasts compiled
High
48
Community launch strategy documented — Product Hunt, relevant Slack groups, LinkedIn, newsletters
High
49
Launch day amplification plan: email blast timing, social posting schedule, partner co-announcements
High
50
Post-launch retro scheduled for Day 7, Day 30, and Day 90 — owner and format defined
