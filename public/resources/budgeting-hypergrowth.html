01 · Foundation

The growth equation

Every marketing budget should be built from first principles, not from "what did we spend last year." Start with the revenue target and work backwards.

The Core Revenue Model
Revenue = Leads × Conversion Rate × ACV − Churn
Work backwards from your ARR target. If you need $3M ARR with an ACV of $12,000, you need 250 customers. At a 5% MQL → Closed Won rate, you need 5,000 MQLs. At a 3% website → MQL rate, you need 167,000 website sessions. Now you can budget.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

CAC = Total Sales & Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers Won

Always calculate CAC per channel. Blended CAC hides which channels are profitable and which are destroying value.

Benchmarks for B2B SaaS:   Good: <$500   Acceptable: $500–$2,000   Danger: >$2,000

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin ÷ Monthly Churn Rate

Use gross margin, not revenue. A $10K ARR customer with 40% gross margin has an LTV of $4K — not $10K. Know the difference.

LTV:CAC Target:   Poor: <1×   Acceptable: 1–3×   Healthy: 3–5×   Elite: >5×

Payback Period

Payback = CAC ÷ (ARPU × Gross Margin %)

How many months until a new customer pays back their acquisition cost. Investors scrutinise this at every fundraise.

Payback Benchmarks:   <12 months: Strong   12–24 months: Acceptable   >24 months: Fix urgently

Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER)

MER = Total Revenue ÷ Total Marketing Spend

A blended efficiency metric useful for boards. Tracks every dollar of marketing spend against total revenue generated — across all channels.

MER Benchmarks:   Poor: <3×   Acceptable: 3–5×   Strong: >5×

05 · Red Flags

Warning signs your budget model is broken

⚠

Blended CAC is your only CAC metric

If you're only tracking total spend ÷ total customers, you have no idea which channels are profitable. You're almost certainly scaling your worst-performing channels.

⚠

LTV:CAC below 2× and you're scaling paid spend

Below 2×, every customer you acquire destroys value faster than you can recover it. Fix economics before scaling. No amount of growth fixes a broken unit model.

⚠

Marketing budget set as a % of last year's revenue

Budgeting backward from history is how you guarantee you'll repeat history. Build from revenue targets and work backward through the funnel instead.

⚠

No payback period tracking at channel level

A channel with a 30-month payback period is burning cash you won't see returned for 2.5 years. Without tracking this, you can't make intelligent capital allocation decisions.
